\section{Reading the Tables}
The tables report selected decoder-side configurations for Pythia-1.4B. Rows are transformer layers, indexed from 0 to 23. The layer-sparsity table reports which sub-blocks are kept in the verified $50\%$ configuration evaluated with approximately $131$k calibration tokens. The quantization and low-rank tables group the four repeated matrix families per layer: attention output, query-key-value projection, MLP down projection, and MLP up projection.

\begin{tcolorbox}[cvtbox]
\textbf{Layer-sparsity entries} show the kept sub-blocks: attention plus MLP, attention only, MLP only, or full layer drop. \textbf{Quantization levels} are shown directly as bit-widths: 2, 2.5, 3, 4, or 8 bits. \textbf{Low-rank levels} are shown as the retained fraction of singular values: 5\%, 10\%, 20\%, 35\%, 50\%, or 75\%. Darker cells indicate larger retained precision or rank.
\end{tcolorbox}
