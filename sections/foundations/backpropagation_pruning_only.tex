\subsection{Backpropagation}

Backpropagation computes these gradients efficiently by applying the chain rule from the output layer back through the network \cite{Rumelhart1986}. In compact form, it provides the quantities
$\nabla_{\theta}\mathcal{L}$
where $\theta$ denotes the trainable parameters and $\mathcal{L}$ is the loss function.

For this pruning-focused report, the detailed derivation is not needed. What matters is that backpropagation links model error to individual parameters and intermediate structures. This link is used later by gradient-based pruning criteria, Taylor approximations, Fisher-based scores, and recovery stages after structural removal.

\section{Conclusion}

In this chapter we reviewed the mathematical formulation and optimization principles underlying deep neural networks. By parameterizing networks as a sequence of matrix multiplications and formulating training as empirical risk minimization, we showed that there are two primary entities attacked by compression which are the weight matrices and the loss function itself. We saw how the loss function signal propagates through the network during training in the backpropagation step and how optimizers use the signal from each weight to update that parameter's value during the optimization step. This perspective on optimization is critical to compression because the signals it generates are the local (first or second-order) loss derivatives which form the basis of many parameter-importance heuristics used in pruning approaches.

Though the principles above apply to all feedforward networks, natural language processing models, in modern usage, rely on extremely specialized architectures. In order to adapt the mathematical concepts above, we must understand how these matrix operations are tied to specific structures. As such, the next chapter is entirely devoted to detailing the architecture of the Transformer, specifically how it works, where parameters are stored, and why the overwhelming computational overhead it produces necessitate model compression for deployment.
